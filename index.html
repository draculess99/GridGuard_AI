GridGuard Architecture

A deep dive into the underlying data pipelines, forecasting models, and explainable decision intelligence that power GridGuard AI.

🗄️

1. Tri-Source Data Ingestion

Data arrives from three distinct streams: a reproducible offline Synthetic Generator, historical Kaggle CSV uploads, and live balancing-authority demand from the EIA API. All incoming data is instantly validated.

🔄

2. Canonical Schema Normalization

Raw sequential data undergoes strict normalization. Timestamps are aligned to UTC, missing hours are interpolated or dropped, and a consistent schema is enforced so downstream models remain source-independent.

📈

3. XGBoost Forecasting & SHAP

The normalized sequence is expanded into autoregressive lags and rolling statistics. An XGBoost model produces a recursive 12–48 hour forecast, while a SHAP TreeExplainer extracts transparent feature importances.

⚙️

4. Deterministic Expert Rules

Forecast evidence is fed into a hard-coded expert system. It evaluates capacity pressure, high-risk hours, and demand shocks, generating a fired-rule trace and an objective confidence score.

🧠

5. X-Decision & Local RAG

Grid policy documents are vectorized using TF-IDF. Relevant governance chunks are retrieved (RAG) and combined with the forecast and expert rules into a unified, grounded briefing—with optional LLM summarization.

👤

6. Human-in-the-loop Governance

No decision is executed autonomously. Every recommendation remains advisory until an operator approves or rejects it with an auditable rationale, saving the final decision to a secure JSON or PostgreSQL ledger.
