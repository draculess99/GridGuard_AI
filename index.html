GridGuard AI

Explainable decision intelligence for modern electric grids. Forecast load, identify peak risks, and keep a human operator firmly in control.

Why do we need GridGuard?

Modern electric grids are under unprecedented stress. Extreme weather events, fluctuating temperatures, and unpredictable demand shocks require operators to make high-stakes decisions rapidly.

Traditional forecasting models provide numbers, but lack context. Fully autonomous AI systems act like "black boxes," making decisions that operators can't audit or trust.

GridGuard AI solves this by combining powerful XGBoost machine learning with a transparent "Expert System." It provides operators with a crystal-clear forecast, evaluates risks using hard-coded governance rules, surfaces relevant grid policies via Local RAG, and ultimately requires human approval for any action.

System Architecture Breakdown

A look at the underlying data pipelines, forecasting models, and decision intelligence.

🗄️

1. Data Ingestion & Normalization

Ingests from Synthetic generators, historical Kaggle CSVs, or live EIA balancing-authority APIs. Normalizes all timestamps and interpolates missing hours into a canonical schema.

📈

2. XGBoost & SHAP Forecasting

Predicts 12–48 hours of future demand using rolling and autoregressive lag features. Utilizes SHAP values to explain exactly which features (like temperature or time of day) drove the forecast.

⚙️

3. Expert Risk Engine

Translates the forecast into operational risk. A deterministic, hard-coded rules engine evaluates capacity pressure and demand shocks, generating a fired-rule trace and an objective confidence score.

🧠

4. X-Decision & Local RAG

Uses TF-IDF to retrieve relevant governance policies (Local RAG). Combines the forecast, risk rules, and retrieved policies into a grounded briefing, with optional LLM (Grok/Groq/Gemini) summarization.

👤

5. Human-in-the-loop (HITL)

No decision executes autonomously. Operators review the evidence in the Streamlit Control Tower and must explicitly approve or reject the recommendation with an auditable rationale.

🛡️

6. Audit & Persistence

Approved actions and their rationales are stored securely in a local JSON ledger or a remote PostgreSQL database to ensure strict regulatory compliance and historical review.

Operational Capabilities

GridGuard is designed as a decision-support control tower rather than an autonomous grid controller.

🌡️

Weather & Demand Scenarios

Stress-test the forecast against heatwaves, cold snaps, demand surges, generation loss, and reduced reserve capacity.

  • Adjust temperature and load assumptions
  • Compare baseline and stressed outcomes
  • Surface changes in reserve margin
🚨

Peak-Risk Detection

Converts raw forecasts into clear operating signals by assessing capacity pressure, demand shocks, and confidence.

  • Low, guarded, elevated, and critical states
  • Fired-rule trace for every recommendation
  • Escalation when evidence is uncertain
🔎

Explainable Recommendations

Pairs model output with SHAP feature attribution, deterministic rules, and retrieved policy evidence.

  • Top forecast drivers
  • Relevant operating-policy excerpts
  • Plain-language decision briefings
